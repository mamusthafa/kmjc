Sub: Proposed waqf (amendment) bill 2024.

Sir,

The waqf amendment bill 2024 as placed before Loksabha and now under consideration of JPC is detrimental to the interest of Muslim community. I oppose the same in following points.

1. The proposed waqf (amendment) act 2024 basically opposes many terms and clauses of existing Waqf act 1995. These are detrimental to interests of Muslims.

2. Existing provisions 1(1), 3(i), (p), (r),; r(i), (iv), 4(1), (1A), (2), (3), a to f, (4)(5), (6), 6(1), 7(1), 13(2-A), 14(6), 14(8), 16(d), 20(1)(a), (b), (c), (2), 23(1), 32(2)(e)explanation ,(3), (6), 36(3)(f),(4), (7), 37(1)(f),40(1)(2)(3), 46(2), 48(4), 52(4), 52(2),52(A)(1), 55A(2), 67(6), 83(4)a,b,c, 83(7), 83(9), 101, 104, 107, 108, 108A, 109, 110 are to be retained. Modifications proposed are to be deleted.

3. New insertion in the bill as proposed in d(da), 3B(i), 3C(i), 32(2), (3), (4), 5(2A), 9(2), 36(a), (1A),7(A), 10, 37(a), 47(a), (b), 48(a), 50(d),(f),(i), 61(b)(ii), 91(4)(iii), 100, 108B1(1) of the existing act/provisions are to be deleted.

4. In many provisions which are exclusively set apart of members of Muslim community, proposal to include non-Muslims is to be avoided. For example, sections 7(v),(vi)(ii), 14 etc.

5. Since our constitution envisages right for belief and religion, the waqf rendered by Muslims shall continue as such and to be governed by Muslims only,
